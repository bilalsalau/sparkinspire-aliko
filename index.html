Young People Can Change The World

They are doing it already


From 2012 the population of Nigeria has grown from around 182 million to an estimated population of over 200.96 million as of 2019, with more than 50% under 30 years of age, putting a severe strain on a nation already suffering from a slowing economy, high unemployment rate and declining revenue to provide enough schools and health facilities.

Nigeria is the most populous country in Africa, yet we have the highest rate of extreme poverty. This statistics can’t be ignored. The one thing that I have wondered is that if there are so many young people in Nigeria, then why aren’t we doing anything about it? And what actions can we be taking to make our home - Nigeria- a better place for all its citizens.

The UN, for statistical consistency across regions, define youth, as those person between the ages of 15 to 24 thus making secondary schools in Nigeira a wealth well of unprecedented potential for economic and social progress. Too many, and I mean too many of Nigeria’s young people have their potentials hindered by extreme poverty, discrimination or lack of information. But with proper investment in their education and opportunities, these young people’s ideas, ideals and innovations could transform the future of our nation. Many times the young people in Nigeria are looked upon as beneficiaries but it’s time we looked at young people as partners and involve them in the conversations and actions for economic and social progress.


by Bilal Salau | Founder and President Sparkinspire

Our Vision

That the Aliko club spaces in secondary schools have given students the opportunity and platform that have empowered them to engage in the conversations and actions for economic and social progress. They have changed the narrative of what it means to be a Nigerian youth through their passion led sustainable development causes and initiatives that have both addressed the potential impact of unemployment, poor education, poverty, gender inequality and other hinderances of sustainable resources and livelihoods of the Nigerian people.
